# M7 Participant Recruitment Brief

## Who We Need

Three independent developers or product-focused builders who have chosen software side projects and did not help build AI Idea Hunter.

## Invitation

> I’m testing AI Idea Hunter, a local-first workspace for evaluating software ideas. I’m looking for a 30–40 minute usability session where you use a real but non-confidential idea, share your screen, and explain what you expect. We are testing the product, not your ability. Participation is voluntary, you can stop at any time, and only anonymized notes will be added to the public project. No recording is planned by default.

## Screening Questions

1. Have you chosen or evaluated a software project in the last year?
2. What do you currently use to capture or compare ideas?
3. Did you help design, implement, or review AI Idea Hunter?
4. Can you use a sanitized idea without employer, client, or personal secrets?
5. Are you comfortable with anonymized task notes being synthesized in a public repository?

Eligible participants answer yes to questions 1, 4, and 5, and no to question 3. Experience with similar tools is useful context, not a pass/fail criterion.

## Scheduling Checklist

- Assign the next unused code from P01 to P03.
- Send the public demo link only when the session begins if first-impression evidence matters.
- Ask the participant to use a clean browser profile when practical.
- Confirm note consent before tasks and quote consent separately.
- Keep names, contact details, calendar links, and raw notes outside the repository.
